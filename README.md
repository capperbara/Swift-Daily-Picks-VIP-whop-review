## Understanding the Bet Types Swift Daily Picks VIP Covers

  

To get full value from Swift Daily Picks VIP, you should understand the main categories of bets the service provides picks for:

  

### Moneyline Bets

  

Moneyline picks are the simplest: you're picking which team wins outright, no spread involved.

  

A moneyline pick might say **"Celtics -200"** (bet $200 to win $100) or **"Heat +160"** (bet $100 to win $160).

  

The favored team pays lower odds, while the underdog pays higher odds.

  

Swift Daily Picks cheat sheets often include moneyline picks as your most straightforward option if you're just starting out.

  

### Point Spread Bets

  

Point spread picks adjust the score by a handicap to level the playing field.

  

If Swift Daily Picks says **"Mahomes Over 1.5 Passing TDs -110,"** they're giving you a pick that the Chiefs QB throws for at least 2 touchdown passes.

  

The -110 means you risk $110 to win $100 (standard sportsbook juice).

  

This is the most common bet type in American sports betting, accounting for the majority of daily action.

  

### Over/Under Totals

  

Over/Under picks focus on the combined score from both teams, not the winner.

  

A cheat sheet might highlight **"Lakers vs Suns Total Over 217.5"** (both teams combine to score over 217 points).

  

If the combined final score is 215, the under wins; if it's 220, the over wins.

  

Most bettors combine over/under picks with moneylines or spreads to build parlay tickets.

  

### Parlay Picks

  

A parlay combines multiple picks (at least 2) where all must hit for the bet to win.

  

Swift Daily Picks might give you a **3-game parlay** combining spreads and moneylines, with a potential 6:1 payout if all three hit.

  

Parlays are appealing because they pay out much more than individual bets, but they're also harder to hit (all legs must win or the whole ticket loses).

  

In the USA, parlays account for upward of 70% of each state's total sportsbook revenue each month—they're incredibly popular.

  

### Teaser Bets

  

A teaser is a modified parlay that gives you better odds on each individual leg in exchange for a lower overall payout.

  

Instead of a 3-game parlay at 6:1, you might get a teaser at 5:1 with the spread adjusted in your favor.

  

For example, a 3-point favorite becomes a 9-point favorite in your favor, making it easier to hit.

  

Swift Daily Picks occasionally includes teaser recommendations for bettors who want slightly better odds.

  

### Proposition Bets (Props)

  

Props are specific statistical outcomes, not related to the final score.

  

A cheat sheet might pick **"Travis Kelce Over 6.5 Receptions"** or **"Jalen Brunson Under 2.5 Three-Pointers Made."**

  

Props are popular because they let you target individual player performance rather than team outcomes.

  

Swift Daily Picks includes prop picks regularly, especially during major events like the Super Bowl.

  

### Futures Bets

  

Futures picks are long-term wagers measured in weeks or months, like betting on a team to win the Super Bowl or a player to win MVP.

  

A future pick might be placed in September at 50:1 odds for a team to win the championship in February.

  

Swift Daily Picks occasionally includes futures recommendations, though these are less frequent than daily live betting picks.

  

### Live Betting Picks

  

Live betting (in-play betting) picks are placed while a game is in progress.

  

The odds change constantly based on game flow, so Swift Daily Picks VIP gives you early notifications for live betting opportunities.

  

A pick might say **"Under 100.5 Total Points (Q2 only)"** if the game is playing lower than expected.

  

This is where the **early notification system** becomes crucial—you need to act fast.
